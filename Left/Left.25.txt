Shiveluch volcano spews kilometer-high ash cloud
The Shiveluch volcano on the Kamchatka Peninsula in Russia's Far East has erupted. Authorities have declared the second-highest alert level.

Spectacular images from the Russian Far East: The Shiveluch volcano on the remote Kamchatka Peninsula has erupted, spewing out a huge ash cloud. The ash column from the 3,300-meter-high volcano rose to almost 9,000 meters above sea level, the regional office of the Russian Academy of Sciences announced on Wednesday. Ash was also reportedly hanging in the air more than 100 kilometers away from the volcano

Due to the outbreak, authorities have declared the second-highest alert level. So far, there have been no reports of injuries or damage in the sparsely populated region.

Mount Shiveluch lies approximately 450 kilometers north of Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, the largest city on the peninsula. Its age is estimated at 60,000 to 70,000 years; it is one of the largest and most active volcanoes on the peninsula.
